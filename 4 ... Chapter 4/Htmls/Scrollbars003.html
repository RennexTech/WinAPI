Keyboard Shortcuts: Level Up Your App's Accessibility

Key (VK_ Value) What It Does (The Shortcut Play)
VK_UP

When the user presses the Up Arrow key, your app should handle this by scrolling the content up by one line. Think of it like reading a document and pressing the up arrow to gently nudge the view up, showing the previous line.

This is a super common and intuitive way for users to navigate text or lists without needing a mouse.
VK_DOWN

When the Down Arrow key is pressed, your app should scroll the content down by one line. It's the exact opposite of `VK_UP`, allowing users to move forward through content line by line.

Essential for smooth, controlled reading and navigating through any scrollable area with precision.
VK_PRIOR

This virtual key code corresponds to the Page Up key. When hit, your app should scroll the content up by a full page. Imagine flipping back one page in an e-book instantly.

It's for rapid navigation when you want to see a whole chunk of previous information without having to scroll slowly.
VK_NEXT

This is for the Page Down key. When activated, your app should scroll the content down by a full page. Like skipping ahead a full screen of text or data.

Super useful for quickly Browse through long documents or feeds, jumping to the next visible section.
VK_HOME

When the Home key is pressed, your application should instantly scroll all the way to the very top of the content. Think of it as a "rewind to the beginning" button for your scrollable area.

This saves users from endless scrolling to get back to the start, making navigation super efficient.
VK_END

Hitting the End key should make your app scroll directly to the very bottom of the content. It's the express train to the finish line of any scrollable view.

Perfect for quickly getting to the latest information in a log, chat, or a long document's conclusion.
